You signed the offer. Congratulations — and now the real logistics begin. The first ninety days in a new country set the tone for everything that follows. Here is a grounded, sequenced guide for professionals relocating to Canada.

## Days 1–7: The essentials

Get the foundations in place immediately, because everything else depends on them.

- **Social Insurance Number (SIN)** — required to work and get paid. Apply as soon as you arrive.
- **Bank account** — most major banks offer newcomer packages.
- **Phone plan** — a local number unlocks nearly every other service.
- **Temporary housing** — secure short-term accommodation before committing to a lease.

## Days 8–30: Build your base

With essentials handled, establish the systems of daily life.

1. **Permanent housing** — understand neighborhoods, commute times, and lease norms.
2. **Provincial health coverage** — enrollment timing varies by province, so check early.
3. **Transit and ID** — a provincial ID or driver's licence and a transit pass.

> Settling in is a project. Treat it like one — with a checklist and a sequence — and the overwhelm fades quickly.

## Days 31–60: Find your footing at work

- **Learn the workplace culture.** Canadian workplaces tend to value directness balanced with politeness, and collaboration over hierarchy.
- **Build relationships early.** A coffee with each teammate pays off for months.
- **Clarify expectations** with your manager in your first weeks rather than assuming.

## Days 61–90: Put down roots

- **Build a local network** — professional meetups, alumni groups, community organizations.
- **Set up long-term finances** — credit history starts now, so use credit responsibly.
- **Explore.** The faster a new place feels like home, the better you perform at work.
